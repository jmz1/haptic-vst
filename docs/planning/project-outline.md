This is a research project into the haptic phenomenology of vibratory stimulus, and how it may be applied for deep healing of the nervous system, and cultivation of health and wellbeing.

The project will involve the design and construction of a cushioned table-like device, fitted with up to 32 vibratory transducers, capable of decently powerful vibratory stimulus in a range of approximately 20 to 200 Hz.

A variety of methods and software will be developed to generate patterns of stimulus over all 32 channels in real time, in response to inputs including audio signals and MIDI notes.

The foundational method for these stimulus generation techniques will involve the spatial translation of a stimulus defined in an abstract space (such as a 2D plane or torus, or even a representation of the somatosensory connectome) into the individual outputs of each transducer, by giving each transducer a coordinate within that space, and spatially sampling the contents of the space at that location.

Other processing and translation layers may be present, for example to allow for multiple body plan configurations, to allow for heterogenous transducer types, to superimpose multiple stimuli, and to normalise the output to sensory sensitivity in that region.

One compelling concept for producing these stimuli within an abstract 2D space is the direct simulation of a 2D wave model. This would allow for the simulation of spaces with defined (or possibly varying in time and space) velocity and damping characteristics.

Depending on the sophistication of the model, it may be possible to capture wave effects such as dispersion, or the Doppler effect.

It remains to be determined what kind of wave model will be computationally practical for real time use. Determining this is a top research priority.

One possibility for a computationally efficient model suitable for real-time use is a phasor representation. This appears suitable for fixed-frequency stimuli, but it is unknown if full wave effects such as Doppler or dispersion can be captured with methods based on this.
